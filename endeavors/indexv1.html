  • Affective Clinical Neuroscience Laboratory

    October 2013-Present

    I work as a research assistant in the lab. My work as of yet has mostly centered on investigation of reward-related processing via electroencephalography (EEG), using the method of Event Related Potentials (ERP) to investigate electrical responses to reward-related stimuli. I am also involved in similar work that uses funtional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI).

    This past summer, I used these methods to investigate the interaction between Gratification-Delay and relative anticipation/reactivity to reward. This project was funded by the Howard Hughes Medical Institute via the competitively awarded NU BioScientist grant. The proposal for the work can be found here, and a poster summarizing my findings can be found here.

  • Northwestern University Model United Nations (NUMUN)

    September 2013-Present

    NUMUN is an annual Model United Nations conference for high school students organized by Northwestern students. I serve as Secretary-Parliamentarian for NUMUN XI, the conference to be held in April of 2015. This position entails creation/optimization of parliamentary debating procedure for the conference, as well as general staff management.

    For NUMUN X this past year, I served as a committee chair, organizing a simulation of the International Court of Justice for ~15 students with a staff of two other students. We wrote topic papers for and managed student debate for three topics: Sino-Japanese Territorial Disputes, Southeast Asian Deforestation, and Whale Conservation.
